Mindfulness Training Northern Ireland

Welcome

I am very fortunate to offer mindfulness training in my mindfulness studio, set in the powerful and peaceful setting of the Mourne Mountains. I also regularly run mindfulness programmes in the city of Newry, which is within easy distance of Armagh, Dundalk, Banbridge and many other population centres.

I am a professionally trained counsellor, psychotherapist and mindfulness trainer. My work is supervised and my training and practice meet internationally recognised standards of best practice. I deliver what is generally considered the gold standard training in mindfulness the eight week Mindfulness Based Stress Reduction programme/ mindfulness based cognitive therapy programme. (MBSR/MBCT).

I am committed to the highest standards of training and delivery so that people in Northern Ireland may have the best possible opportunity to access the transformative potential of mindfulness meditation and practices. I also offer one to one mindfulness coaching and a mindfulness informed counselling and psychotherapy service.

It is a real joy to offer this 'way of life' and to do my best to live it and have my own life enhanced by the joy and peace that over time it has brought to me. You are most welcome to attend anything that I am offering and to make contact with questions.

Warmly,

Deirdre.

Benefits

Whilst this is an ancient practice, it is only in recent decades that science and medicine have come to recognise and prove the capacity of mindfulness to improve the quality of people's lives; helping anyone living with the stress of illness, injury or pain, anyone working with depression, anxiety or recovering from addiction and more broadly anyone wanting to improve their relationship with the daily challenges of stress. It promotes self awareness, self regulation, self compassion and a stable, centred way of being.

What is mindfulness?

Mindfulness is a simple practice that connects us to our natural sense of presence, stability and balance. Mindfulness is a way of being that arises when we pay attention, on purpose, deeply and without judgment, to whatever is occurring in the present moment. It involves adopting a friendly attitude to whatever we notice.
